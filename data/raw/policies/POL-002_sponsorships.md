# POL-002: Sponsorships Policy
**Version:** 1.4
**Effective Date:** 2024-01-01
**Category:** sponsorship
**Owner:** Global Compliance Office

## 1. Purpose
Governs all financial sponsorships of events, organisations,
and activities to prevent misuse as indirect hospitality or
inducement channels.

## 2. Scope
All sponsorship agreements where the organisation provides
financial support to external events or entities.

## 3. Approval Thresholds

| Sponsorship Amount  | Registration  | Approval Required      |
|---------------------|---------------|------------------------|
| Under USD 1,000     | Not required  | Manager                |
| USD 1,000–USD 4,999 | Not required  | Senior Manager         |
| USD 5,000–USD 24,999| **GSMM**      | Director               |
| USD 25,000–USD 49,999| GSMM + GTE  | **VP**                 |
| USD 50,000 and above| GSMM + GTE   | **SVP + Legal review** |

## 4. GSMM Registration
Global Sponsorship Management & Monitoring (GSMM) registration
is mandatory for all sponsorships USD 5,000 and above.
Registration must be completed **before** any commitment is made.

## 5. GTE Registration
Global Third-party Engagement (GTE) registration required for
sponsorships USD 25,000 and above.

## 6. Public Sector Events
Sponsorships where public officials or government representatives
will receive direct benefit (passes, hospitality, gifts):
- Require **Legal review regardless of amount**
- All attendee lists must be reviewed for public official presence
- Country-specific limits apply to any per-person benefit value

## 7. Prohibited Sponsorships
- Events where the primary beneficiary is a public official
- Sponsorships structured to provide personal benefit
  to individuals involved in procurement decisions
- Sponsorships without documented business purpose

## 8. Exception Approval Tool (EAT)
Sponsorships that fall outside standard policy parameters
must be submitted to the Exception Approval Tool before
any commitment or payment.
