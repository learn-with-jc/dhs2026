# POL-005: Public Sector Spending Limits
**Version:** 1.2
**Effective Date:** 2024-01-01
**Category:** meals
**Owner:** Global Compliance + Legal

## 1. Purpose
Establishes country-specific and tier-specific spending limits
for any hospitality, gifts, or entertainment involving
public sector employees, government officials, or
government-funded organisations.

## 2. Why This Policy Exists
Anti-bribery laws (including FCPA, UK Bribery Act, and local
equivalents) impose strict liability for providing value
to government officials. These limits are set conservatively
to ensure legal safety across all jurisdictions.

## 3. Tier Classification

### US Level 1 (Standard)
Applies to: US federal, state, and local government employees
- Meals per person: **USD 50**
- Gifts per occasion: **USD 20**
- No gift cards or cash-equivalent items
- Prior Legal approval for any engagement

### KR Level 2 (Korea — Strict)
Applies to: Korean government employees, public institution staff
- Meals per person: **USD 50** (hard cap, KRW equivalent)
- Gifts per occasion: **USD 20**
- All engagements require **Legal pre-approval**
- Gift cards and vouchers: **PROHIBITED**
- Any engagement over USD 100 total requires **SVP approval**

### IN (India — Standard)
Applies to: Indian government employees and PSU staff
- Meals per person: **USD 25**
- Gifts per occasion: **USD 15**
- All engagements require **Legal pre-approval**

### Default (All Other Countries)
Where no country-specific rule exists:
- Meals per person: **USD 50**
- Gifts per occasion: **USD 20**

## 4. Known Public Officials
When a recipient is a **known public official**
(government minister, procurement officer, regulator):
- Limits above are reduced by **50%**
- Legal + SVP approval required regardless of amount
- GSMM registration mandatory if any sponsorship element present
- All interactions must be logged in the Ethics & Compliance system

## 5. Mandatory Checks
Before any engagement with public sector recipients:
1. Confirm recipient classification (public/private)
2. Apply country-specific threshold
3. Verify no active procurement relationship
4. Submit to Legal for review
5. Log in compliance system

## 6. Zero Tolerance
Gifts, entertainment, or hospitality to public officials
involved in **active procurement or contract award decisions**
are **PROHIBITED regardless of value**.
This is a zero-tolerance provision with no exceptions.
